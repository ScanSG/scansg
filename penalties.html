💰 Fines & Penalties — Don’t Be Siao: Singapore Edition 🚨

🇸🇬 Singapore is clean, safe, and efficient — because rules are rules, and fines hit faster than a durian in monsoon season 🌩️💨. Whether you're a tourist, expat, or local, ignorance is not an excuse. Break the law? The fine will come with a side of shame 🍳 and CCTV footage 📹👀.

Locals follow these rules not because they’re scared, but because it keeps the city running smoothly. And yes — NEA officers patrol like hawks, and CCTV sees all.

So keep your wallet ready — and your gum, vape, and phone in check.

📋 Common Fines in Singapore (2025)

Yes, these apply to tourists too. No exceptions. Reality Check tells you if it’s actively enforced.

⚠️ Offense 💸 Penalty (SGD) 🔍 Reality Check (Enforcement Level)
껌 Chewing Gum (Import/Sale) Up to $100,000 and/or 2 years jail High Enforcement: Customs and NEA actively screen luggage. Even 2 sticks? You’ll be questioned. Selling it? Game over.
🗑️ Littering (Cigarette Butt, Tissue, Food) $300 (1st), up to $1,000 (repeat) Very High Enforcement: NEA officers patrol hawker centres and parks daily. Drop a tissue? Fine issued on the spot.
🚗 Littering from Vehicle $500 High Enforcement: CCTV monitors roads. If caught, all passengers can be fined unless driver names culprit.
🚶‍♂️ Jaywalking $50–$150 Medium Enforcement: Common near MRT exits. Officers issue fines during peak hours. Use the crossing — it’s not hard.
🚭 Smoking in Prohibited Areas $200–$1,000 Very High Enforcement: Hawker centres, parks, MRT stations — strictly no smoking. NEA and town councils enforce heavily.
💨 Vaping / E-Cigarette Use $1,000 (1st), up to $2,000 + jail Very High Enforcement: Full ban since 2020. Possession or use = fine. Confiscated at airports. No exceptions.
🚽 Urine in Elevator / Public Toilet $500–$1,000 Medium Enforcement: Caught via CCTV. Town councils issue fines and may visit your home. Yes, really.
🚇 Eating/Drinking on MRT/LRT $500 Very High Enforcement: Transit officers patrol daily. Even a sip of bubble tea? You’ll get fined. Water only.
🎨 Vandalism (Graffiti, Scratching Cars) Up to $2,000 + 3 years jail + caning Extreme Enforcement: Caning is mandatory. Police take it seriously. Even a scratch on a car = vandalism charge.
💊 Drugs (Even a Tiny Bit) Mandatory rehab, jail, or death Extreme Enforcement: Zero tolerance. Even trace amounts. You will be caught. You will be punished.
🕊️ Feeding Pigeons / Ducks $300–$1,000 Medium Enforcement: NEA runs sting operations in parks. Yes, they hide and watch. Don’t risk it.
🚽 Not Flushing Public Toilet $150 Low-Medium Enforcement: CCTV in some toilets. Fines issued if caught. Be a good human. Flush.
🛒 Illegal Hawking (Street Vending) $1,000–$5,000 High Enforcement: Hawkers without license? Goods confiscated. No warning. No appeal.
🅿️ Parking Violations $40–$320+ Very High Enforcement: Use Parking.sg app. Even 5 minutes over? Fine arrives in days.
🚗 Driving Without Insurance Up to $3,000 or 12 months jail High Enforcement: Checked during traffic stops or accidents. No excuse.
📱 Using Phone While Driving $1,000 + 12 demerit points Very High Enforcement: Traffic cameras catch this daily. Even hands-free? Only Bluetooth allowed.
🪑 Not Wearing Seatbelt (Front/Back) $120 per unsecured occupant High Enforcement: Driver is responsible. Kids without car seats? Automatic fine.
🛂 Overstaying Visa $100–$3,000 + possible caning/deportation Extreme Enforcement: Automatic fine. May be detained, banned from re-entry. Sponsors lose bond money.
💼 Working Without Pass $20,000 fine + 2 years jail (employer), deportation (worker) High Enforcement: Includes freelancing, tutoring, helping a friend’s stall. Tourist visa ≠ work permit.
🛸 Drone Flying (No Permit) Up to $20,000 and/or 12 months jail High Enforcement: CAAS monitors airspace. Even small drones need approval. Flying without permit? You’ll be tracked.
🍺 Public Drunkenness / Disorderly Conduct Up to $1,000 or 3 months jail Medium Enforcement: Police arrest loud or aggressive drunks. Falling asleep on MRT? You might get a warning.
🇸🇬 Disrespecting National Symbols Up to $10,000 and/or 3 years jail High Enforcement: Mocking anthem, flag desecration, refusing pledge — taken very seriously. Avoid political stunts.
💊 Bringing in Illegal Medication Up to $10,000 + 10 years jail High Enforcement: ADHD meds (e.g. Adderall), codeine syrup — all controlled. Declare or check HSA first.
🛑 Queue Jumping (Serious Cases) Up to $1,000 Low Enforcement: Rare, but aggressive cutting (e.g. at immigration) can be charged under public order laws. Locals will scold you first.

🔁 Repeat Offenses? Say Hello to Court 🏛️ (and Possibly Caning 🪢)

  • First time? Maybe a warning 🟡. Second time? Composition fine or court summons ⚖️.
  • Some offenses use a demerit point system ⚠️ — collect enough, and you’ll be explaining yourself to a magistrate 🧑‍⚖️.
  • 🪢 Caning applies for serious stuff like vandalism, drug trafficking, illegal entry, and rape. Yes, even foreigners can be caned (males under 50).
  • ✈️ Deportation is automatic for drug offenses, overstaying, or working illegally. May be banned from re-entry for years.

💡 Pro Tips to Stay Out of Trouble ✅

  • When in doubt, DON’T. 🚫 If there’s a sign, follow it. If unsure, ask.
  • Carry a small trash bag 🗑️ when eating on the go. Be the hero who doesn’t litter. 🦸‍♂️
  • Use Parking.sg app 📱 — no excuses for expired coupons.
  • Download SGSecure 🛡️ — get real-time safety alerts from the government 🚨.
  • Never touch someone else’s car or property 🚫🚗 — scratching a door = vandalism 🎨.
  • Check your meds 💊 at HSA — some ADHD meds are illegal here.
  • Don’t fly drones without permit 🚫🛸 — even a mini one. CAAS is watching 👀.
  • Respect queues 🚶‍♂️🚶‍♀️ — no cutting, even if you’re in a hurry. Chiong later, stand properly now. ⏳

🚨 Emergency & Fine Wisdom (Straight from the Kopitiam): ☕

  • For emergencies: 📞 Police 999, Ambulance/Fire 995 🚑🔥.
  • For fines: Your wallet 💸. Keep it handy — and preferably empty of gum 🚫껌, vapes 🚫💨, and loose trash 🚫🗑️.
  • If you feel like doing something "harmless" but sketchy? Wah, better not. This is Singapore, not a blur holiday. 🌴❌
  • Being savvy 🧠 beats being siao 🤪 (crazy/foolish) every single time.
  • Remember: CCTV sees all 📹👁️. NEA is everywhere 👵. And the system does not forgive. ⚖️

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Fines in Singapore

Is chewing gum illegal in Singapore?

Chewing gum is restricted for sale, but small amounts for personal use are allowed.

Can I smoke in public in Singapore?

Smoking is only allowed in designated areas. Fines apply for smoking in prohibited places.

What happens if I litter in Singapore?

Littering can result in a fine or corrective work order for repeat offenders.
