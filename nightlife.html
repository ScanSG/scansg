🍻 Clarke Quay (Party Central)

  • Zouk Singapore Paid

    Legendary nightclub with world-class DJs and themed nights.

    💡 Hack: Buy table package via Klook — includes entry, bottle, and skip-the-line.

  • Hooters Clarke Quay Paid

    American-style bar & grill with burgers, wings, beer, and friendly service — right on the river.

    💡 Hack: Great for casual group dinners before hitting Zouk or Piano Bar. No strict dress code — more relaxed than clubs.

  • The Working Title Paid

    Craft beer bar with a wide selection of local and international brews.

    💡 Hack: Try their beer flights to sample different flavors. Great for beer lovers.

  • Jumbo Kingdom (Boat Quay) Paid

    Iconic floating restaurant serving chili crab and Cantonese cuisine — just a 2-min walk from Clarke Quay.

    💡 Hack: Visit for sunset cocktails on the deck — amazing river views without the dinner price tag.

  • 1880 Heritage Paid

    Colonial-era open-air bar with live music, food stalls, and cultural events — a favorite for Indian expats and families.

    💡 Hack: Visit on weekends for Bollywood nights and live bands. Great for group outings. No strict dress code — very welcoming.

    🎉 Pro Tip: They host festive events during Diwali, Pongal, and Holi — check their Instagram for dates. Free entry before 8 PM.

🍜 Geylang: Singapore’s Night Food Capital

  • Geylang Lor 9 Rojak Free (Food Paid)

    Famous fruit & vegetable salad with thick shrimp paste.

    💡 Hack: Open late (till 3 AM). Look for long queues — that’s where the best stall is.

  • Geylang Lor 29 Fried Hokkien Mee Free (Food Paid)

    Sizzling wok-fried noodles with prawns and squid.

    💡 Hack: Go before 9 PM — stalls sell out fast.

  • Geylang Durian Stalls (Lor 1 to 33) Free (Food Paid)

    24/7 durian paradise — Musang King, D24, Red Prawn.

    💡 Hack: Bring wet wipes and sit on plastic stools. Cash only.

  • Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple Free

    Beautiful South Indian temple with vibrant gopuram.

    💡 Hack: Visit during evening pujas — bells, incense, and chanting.

  • Geylang Serai Night Market Free (Shopping Paid)

    Bustling Ramadan market with halal food, clothes, and souvenirs.

🚨 Reality Check: Geylang is also known for its red-light district (Lor 6 to 10).
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Tip: Stick to food streets (Lor 9, 29) and temples. Avoid walking alone at night in quiet alleys.
📸 Photo Tip: The neon signs and shophouses are iconic — but be discreet in sensitive areas.

    🍽️ Pro Tip: Grab a drink at Alchemy Bar inside the casino — no minimum spend for non-gamblers.
    🚫 No Photography: Strictly enforced on the gaming floor.

  • Resorts World Sentosa Casino Paid (Entry Fee for SG/MY)

    Island-based casino with luxury lounges and high-roller suites.

    💡 Hack: Located inside RWS. Same levy rules apply for locals. Free entry for foreigners (21+).

    🚇 Access: Enter via Cable Car or Sentosa Boardwalk to avoid traffic.

🚨 Nightlife Safety & Transport Hacks

  • MRT Closures

    MRT stops at 12:30 AM. Use Grab, Gojek, or night buses after.

  • Dress Code

    Clubs: Smart casual. No slippers. Some reject shorts.

  • Alcohol Prices

    Expect $18–$25 for cocktails. Happy hours: 5–8 PM.

  • Safety Zones

    Stick to Clarke Quay, Sentosa, Marina Bay. Avoid Geylang red-light district.
