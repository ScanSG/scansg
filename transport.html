üöï Taxi & Ride-Hailing (Grab, Gojek, Comfort & More)

Comfortable, but pricier. Best for late nights, bad weather, or group travel. But don‚Äôt just open Grab ‚Äî **use multiple apps** to compare prices and avoid surge.

  • Starting Fare: ~$3.50 (metered taxis), varies for ride-hailing
  • Providers:

    • Gojek: Often cheaper than Grab. Offers GoCar, GoRide (e-scooter rental), and food delivery.
    • ComfortDelGro (CDG): Traditional taxi company. Use their app to book metered taxis or private hires (ComfortTrans).
    • CDG Premier: Premium service with newer cars. Slightly higher base fare.
    • Strides: Formerly SMRT Taxis. Reliable, good app, integrates with public transport data.
    • TADA: Budget-friendly, no surge pricing. Great for short trips.
  • No Motorbike Taxis: Unlike other Southeast Asian cities, Singapore does NOT have motorbike taxis (e.g., GrabBike for passengers). ‚ÄúGrabBike‚Äù here is for scooter rentals only.
  • ERP Charges: Taxis pay these ‚Äî you don‚Äôt get charged extra for entering ERP zones.
  • Tipping: Not expected. Rounding up is polite but not required.
  • Surge Pricing: High during rain, peak hours (7:30‚Äì9:30 AM, 5:30‚Äì7:30 PM), and events. Compare Grab, Gojek, and TADA before booking.

Local Hack: In heavy rain, use Gojek or TADA ‚Äî they‚Äôre often cheaper than Grab during surge. And always confirm the pickup point. Some drivers go to the wrong terminal at Changi.
